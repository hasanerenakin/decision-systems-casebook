# Problem Brief

## Current Situation

The university operates twelve shuttle routes connecting campuses,
dormitories and central transportation points.

Vehicle allocation is planned using fixed schedules that are reviewed only
at the beginning of each academic term.

## Observed Problems

1. Morning services frequently exceed passenger capacity.
2. Evening services have low utilization.
3. Delays are recorded manually and inconsistently.
4. Student complaints are not connected to route performance data.
5. Managers cannot compare planned and actual service levels.
6. Vehicle changes are made reactively.

## Business Impact

- Lower student satisfaction
- Increased operational costs
- Poor vehicle utilization
- Unpredictable travel times
- Repeated administrative complaints
- Difficulty justifying fleet decisions

## Target Outcome

Create a system that converts operational data into clear vehicle allocation,
schedule and service improvement recommendations.

## Success Criteria

- Reduce overcrowded trips
- Improve average seat utilization
- Reduce recurring route delays
- Provide traceable recommendations
- Make route performance measurable
