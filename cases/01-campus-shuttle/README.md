# Campus Shuttle Decision System

## Overview

This case study designs a decision-support system for managing university
shuttle routes, vehicle capacity and service frequency.

The proposed system helps transportation managers decide:

- Which routes require additional vehicles
- Which time slots experience excessive demand
- Which services have low capacity utilization
- Which routes consistently experience delays
- When schedules should be revised

## Business Problem

The university currently plans shuttle services using static schedules and
manual observations.

This creates several operational problems:

- Overcrowded vehicles during peak hours
- Underutilized vehicles during low-demand periods
- Repeated delays on specific routes
- Student complaints without structured categorization
- Limited visibility into route performance
- Decisions based on intuition instead of measurable criteria

## Proposed Solution

The system will combine schedule, capacity, passenger and delay information
to generate operational recommendations.

Example recommendations:

- Add one vehicle to Route A between 08:00 and 09:00
- Reduce Route C frequency after 18:00
- Investigate recurring delays at the Central Campus stop
- Increase the minimum transfer time between Route B and Route D

## Main Users

- Transportation operations manager
- Shuttle dispatch coordinator
- University administration
- Student affairs department
- Reporting and analytics team

## Planned Outputs

- Route capacity dashboard
- Peak-hour detection
- Vehicle allocation recommendations
- Delay performance reports
- Service-level alerts
- Student complaint analysis
